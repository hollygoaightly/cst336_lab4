Venus

Venus is the second closest planet to our sun and is the second brightest object in Earth's night sky aside from the moon.

Aside from Uranas, Venus is the only other planet to rotate in the opposite direction as the others, meaning the sun sets in the east and rises in the west.

Venus' solar lap is shorter than Earth's by over 100 days, and a single day on venus equates to 116 days and 18 hours on earth, meaning a year on Venus is less than two of its full days.

Venus' atmosphere is incredibly dense, pressure at its surface is 92 times what it is on earth's surface.

The name Venus comes from the roman goddess of love and beauty.
